# Algebra: Polynomials and Factoring

## Factor Theorem
If P(a) = 0, then (x - a) is a factor of P(x).

## Remainder Theorem
When P(x) is divided by (x - a), the remainder is P(a).

## Common Factoring Identities
- a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)  [Difference of squares]
- a³ - b³ = (a - b)(a² + ab + b²)  [Difference of cubes]
- a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² - ab + b²)  [Sum of cubes]
- (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
- (a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b²
- (a + b)³ = a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³
- (a - b)³ = a³ - 3a²b + 3ab² - b³

## Polynomial Division
Use synthetic division or long division to divide polynomials.

## Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
Every polynomial of degree n has exactly n roots (counting multiplicity) in the complex numbers.

## Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to account for all terms when expanding
- Sign errors in difference of cubes
- Not checking for common factors before applying identities
